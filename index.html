We are looking for new PhD students for a NSF project on human-robot interactions. For prospective students, please see details here.
Mission:
The central objective of our studies is to develop necessary engineering design principles for learning machines (e.g., neural networks) and intelligent systems composed of such machines (e.g., self-driving cars). Specifically, we study
  • (1) methods for identifying worst-case risks (e.g., corner cases in self-driving and adversarial attacks against vision systems),
  • (2) theories for enabling competence awareness of learning machines and intelligent systems (e.g., self-awareness of driving ability during a heavy storm),
  • and (3) new education tools to prepare future engineers and designers with knowledge to conduct responsible risk specifications and design for intelligent engineering products.
Challenges lie across disciplines including system engineering, statistical learning, optimization, and optimal control. Real-world values of our research include socially adept human-robot interactions, accelerated computational material discovery, and robust machine learning.
